SGPA Calculation Masterclass

RGUKT Academic Standard

SGPA Formula

SGPA = (Sum of Credit Points) ÷ (Total Credits)

Credit Points = Grade Point × Credits for that subject

Total Credits = Sum of credits of all subjects in that semester

Step-by-Step Calculation Process

1
List all your subjects for the semester along with their credits and grades.
2
Convert grades to grade points using the RGUKT table (EX=10, A=9, B=8, C=7, D=6, E=5, F=0).
3
Calculate credit points for each subject by multiplying Grade Point × Credits.
4
Sum all credit points obtained in Step 3.
5
Sum all credits of subjects taken in that semester.
6
Divide total credit points by total credits to get your SGPA.

10 Tips to Improve Your SGPA

Prioritize high-credit subjects (4.0 credits).
Aim for EX in labs (easier to score).
Maximize Internal Marks (40% of total).
Review previous years' RGUKT papers.
Maintain 80%+ attendance for concept clarity.
Don't neglect Audit courses.
Use Active Recall for theory subjects.
Avoid backlogs (they cap your GPA later).
Join peer groups for complex math/AI logic.
Be consistent; study 2 hours every day.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q1: What if I have a backlog (failed subject)? Answer: Failed subjects (Grade F) get 0 grade points, which will lower your SGPA. You'll need to clear the backlog in subsequent semesters. The improved grade will be considered for CGPA calculation.
Q2: How is SGPA different from CGPA? Answer: SGPA measures performance for ONE semester, while CGPA (Cumulative GPA) is the average of all semesters combined. CGPA = Total Credit Points of All Semesters ÷ Total Credits of All Semesters.
Q3: Can SGPA be greater than 10? Answer: No. Since the maximum grade point is 10 (EX grade), the maximum possible SGPA is also 10.00.
Q4: Do all subjects have equal credits? Answer: No. Theory subjects typically have 3-4 credits, lab subjects have 1.5-2 credits, and some subjects may have different credit values based on contact hours.
